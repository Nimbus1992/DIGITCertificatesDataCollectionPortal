# Company Context: eGov Foundation

## Who We Are

eGov Foundation is a mission-driven technology organization that builds and maintains **DIGIT** — an open-source Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) platform for urban governance. eGov works with governments across the Global South to digitize core municipal services, with a focus on improving revenue, compliance, transparency, and citizen experience.

eGov operates at the intersection of technology, policy, and governance reform. Our products are deployed through a mix of direct partnerships, system integrator (SI) partnerships, and increasingly through a SaaS model designed for faster, lower-friction country entry.

## What We Build

**DIGIT** is eGov's flagship platform. It is a modular, open-source platform designed for public-sector use cases including:

- **License & Permit Management (LPM)** — Digitizing trade licenses, building permits, and other high-volume recurring government authorizations
- **Complaint Management System (CMS/PGR)** — Citizen grievance redressal
- **Property Tax, Water & Sanitation, and other municipal services**

The platform is configurable (not customizable), built for multi-tenant deployments, and supports centralized upgrade management across jurisdictions.

## How We Go to Market

eGov has historically operated in **project/implementation mode** — large, bespoke government deployments often tied to donor-funded programs (World Bank, AfDB, ADB, EU). We are now transitioning toward **product-led expansion** via a SaaS model.

Our go-to-market relies on:
- **Implementation Partners / System Integrators** who host, configure, and deliver DIGIT to government clients
- **Donor-funded programs** that embed DIGIT modules within larger digital governance reform projects
- **Direct government engagement** in select markets

## Geographic Focus

Primary markets: **Africa** (East, West, and Southern Africa) and **South Asia**. Active conversations in Djibouti, Zambia, Lesotho, Benin, Gabon, Ghana, Sao Tome, Guinea-Bissau, and early-stage interest in LATAM (Mexico, Colombia, Brazil).

## Our Strategic Shift

eGov is moving from **implementation-led** to **product-led expansion**. The SaaS model for DIGIT LPM is the first formal expression of this shift — standardizing deployment, reducing time-to-value, and enabling partner-hosted, multi-country rollouts without requiring eGov to manage each implementation directly.

## Key Relationships

- **eGov Global**: The partner entity expected to host and operate the first SaaS instance
- **SI Partners**: Regional system integrators who bring government relationships and local delivery capacity; eGov provides the product, training, and technical support
- **Donors**: World Bank, AfDB, ADB, EU — primary financiers of government digitization programs in target markets
- **Governments**: Municipal, state, and national entities in the Global South seeking to digitize licensing and permits as part of broader governance reform

## Organizational Mode

eGov is internally transitioning from project-mode delivery (custom per client) to product-mode operations (single global roadmap, tenant-level configuration, centralized releases). This requires new internal capacity across product management, commercial operations, support, and partner enablement.
